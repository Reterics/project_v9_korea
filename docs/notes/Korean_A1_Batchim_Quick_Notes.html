Korean A1 – Batchim & Spelling Quick Notes

1) Core Principle

Korean spelling is morphological (root-based), not purely phonetic.

• What you hear ≠ always what you write. • Spelling preserves the dictionary root.

Mental shift: Word → remember spelling (not sound → build letters).

2) Batchim Simplification

At syllable end, Korean reduces to 7 final sounds:

ㄱ, ㄴ, ㄷ, ㄹ, ㅁ, ㅂ, ㅇ

Double batchim (겹받침): usually only one consonant is pronounced (the rest “hides” unless a vowel follows).

3) Why Duolingo Feels Unfair

  • Often tests spelling from audio without explaining rules.
  • Many different spellings can share the same sound (like English right/write).
  • Solution: learn the word + its dictionary spelling.

4) Essential Double Batchim Patterns (A1)

Written Final sound Example (meaning) Sounds like
ㄹㄱ ㄱ 읽다 (to read) 익따
ㄴㅈ ㄴ 앉다 (to sit) 안따
ㄴㅎ ㄴ 많다 (to be many) 만타
ㅂㅅ ㅂ 없다 (to not have) 업따
ㄹㅁ ㅁ 삶 (life) 삼
ㄹㅂ ㄹ 짧다 (to be short) 짤따

Note: Some minimal sound differences exist, but for beginners, treat these as “same audio, different spelling” cases.

5) Common Confusions

  • 읽다 (read) vs 익다 (ripen) → often sound similar.
  • 앉다 (sit) vs 안 (not) → context decides.
  • 많이 → 마니 (ㅎ disappears).
  • 없다 → 업따 (ㅂㅅ → ㅂ sound).

6) Practical Strategy

  1. Identify the word (meaning + context).
  2. Recall the dictionary form spelling.
  3. Spell from the root (not from sound).

Key reminder: Spelling protects structure. Pronunciation simplifies sound.
